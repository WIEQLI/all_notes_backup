Hi Richard,

We've met and spoken a few times at Mensa functions in San Diego; although, I let my membership lapse 8 or 10 years ago not of disinterest, more a combination of procrastination and poor financial position (which of these is a consequence of the other is hard to say in my case so I list them together). I recall the incident rather than the content of our first conversation over and regarding a chest full of chilled, orange soda. The presence of this elixir was so pervasive and conspicuous at my first Mensa meeting and coupled with our effervescent conversation, that ever since, I can't avoid the suspicion, whenever I encounter Orange Crush among the selections at an event, that the Host is a member of the fraternity of Genius; or at the very least, Awesomeness in one or more of their faculties, Athough this hypothesis has many times been disproven, it nevertheless pervades. For these sublime moments of excitement and anticipation, I have you to thank.

Oh, and I love your books. I have "The Cunning Linguist" and "The Write Way."

I am a Jazz vocalist and entertainer in San Diego and I recently wrote a book that is the first in a series called Napkin Diaries. Basically they're hyper streamlinesd types of micro poetry of philosophical, spiritual and provocative observations which began as scribbles on cocktail napkins (and other scraps of readily available material). I then brutally edit them to poetic simplicity and re-scribe these back on to clean napkins with an 1941 Underwood Typewriter. I then get out the Macro Photography kit and present the photos on Instagram and other social media.
www.instagram.com/napkindiary

I'd really like to sit down with you for a minute, with or without a beverage to ask for your opinion and your advice. I'd really like to send you a copy of the first one which I self-published about a year ago. It's a picture book admittedly; but, the pictures are of words and I thought you might really dig that.

Please let me know your address so I can send you a book, or drop it off and if you've got a minute to talk or grab lunch or something.

David Patrone
