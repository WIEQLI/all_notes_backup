Looking back, he just laughed. One of those laughs that morph into a sobbing cry of pure gratitude. Those were what his friends called "God Shots," messages from the universe, or maybe they were just from his subconscious; when after a period of spiritual drought, the rains came and he knew he would be alright. He was taken care of. How different his life was from what he had imagined. He had learned from the great master, Pain, to acknowledge and release his expectations. Today he was an aficionado of the feelings he used to fear. Today he looked forward with curious wonder at the man he would become. He also wondered what amazing souls he would get to share this Journey with along the way.

How wonderful it is to watch his friends and family, busy manifesting their dreams; daring the heights, paving the way, seizing the day.

It didn't surprise him that the insect with 16 legs on the ground needed to go through such a drastic metamorphosis in order to fly. It also came as no surprise that ten of those legs weren't even really legs. They were what would become the tips of wings. Legs of the air. It reminded him of a poem he'd read once in Portuguese from that; roughly translated, went something like this:

Wings are a burden
To those who are
afraid to fly.

and a Haiku for you

Wings are Heavy Things
when carrying them around
Fearful Of Flying
