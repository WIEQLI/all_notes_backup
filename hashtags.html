My Typewriter: The Creed of a Former US Marine

Adapted From "Rifleman's Creed" by  Major General William H. Rupertus (USMC, Ret.)
(written following the attack on Pearl Harbor)

This is my typewriter. There are many like it, but this one is mine.

My typewriter is my best friend. It is my life. I must master it as I must master my life.

My typewriter, without me, is useless. Without my typewriter, I am useless. I must type my message true. I must write clearer than my enemy (fear) who is trying to silence me. I must reveal my truth before the enemy silences me. I will

My typewriter and myself know that what counts in this war is not the volume of our prose, the clacking of our keys, nor the ink we spill. We know that it is the truths that count. We will hit the truth.

My typewriter is human, even as I, because it is my life. Thus, I will learn it as a brother. I will learn its weaknesses, its strength, its parts, its accessories, its carriage and its keys. I will ever guard it against the ravages of weather and damage as I will ever guard my legs, my arms, my eyes and my heart against damage. I will keep my typewriter clean and ready. We will become part of each other. We will

Before God, I swear this creed. My typewriter and myself are the defenders of my country. We are the masters of our enemy. We are the saviors of my life.

So be it, until victory is America's and there is no enemy, but peace!

The tools
with which
I will play
today

Show me your tools
I'm hauling lumber
one Napkin at a time
