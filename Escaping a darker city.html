She looked out of place;
and, like she belonged
depending on your angle.
She was more or less like
the rest of the room:
relocating, regrouping,
Escaping a Darker City;
some heavy Circumstance;
but, not quite willing
to throw the life away;
cross the bridge to shore.
Safety felt like giving in.
Proud of her scars,
she still craves to taste,
in absence of light,
each single spark,
like an exploding sun.
