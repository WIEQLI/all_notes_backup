hurt people

Created:1/23/2018 12:55 PM
Updated:1/24/2018 2:15 AM

hurt people, hurt people
scared people, scare people
angry people, anger people
loved people, love people




there is a worldwide conspiracy
looking back, they tried to let me in
early on, indoctrinate me
and of course I bought in
only, it wasn't long before I had questions
I saw descrepancies, inconsistencies 
between what we said and what we do
and when I asked I heard the truth
from family and strangers alike
coming out from in between
questions of their own which
they had never asked
in the voices of my family
i heard surprise as if this
was the first time they'd had to lie
and say, "well, yes, that's wrong but" 
"thats just the way it is"  or
"its better than it used to be"
and adding, "soon it'll change, just worry and see." 
til someone comes along and breaks the chains
mean while we don't offend, we ease the pains
in the others and in ourselves
and in that order
but the whole thing rubbed me wrong
unsatisfied I kept on asking
pastor, priest, rabbi, monk
in 40 years, not once did one explain
in a way to satisfy my inner complaint
ans so i turned from God
to science and those who claimed
that puzzles had not intelligent design
that the framework evolved and
intelligence was human, not divine
and such certainty was just as much a puzzle
considering the gaps between found and had yet to find
40 years in search of wind as David's father chimed,
10 or 5 thousands of years ago.
the same paths I wondered,  he walked
and called each endeavor meaningless
with endless resources to spend
to finish each permutation to ending
and called them all a whine, a breeze



playing a game where you have to make mistakes but you have to make the wrong mistakes against an opponent for which rules are only his tool and your obstacle


I have been preparing to make a point, i was hoping not to have to accomplish this posthumously. 

excuse me, but does your dog bite?
no
(gets bit)
apparently I forgot to ask if this was your dog.

Herbert Spencer

Herbert Spencer (27 April 1820 – 8 December 1903) was an English philosopher, biologist, anthropologist, sociologist, and prominent classical liberal political theorist of the Victorian era.
Spencer is best known for the expression "survival of the fittest", which he coined in Principles of Biology (1864), after reading Charles Darwin's On the Origin of Species.
