Silence

Created:1/25/2018 11:05 AM
Updated:1/25/2018 1:21 PM
Author:Lisa Mitchell Parker


My eight year old heartbeat stopped
the moment his muscles tensed, then
sprung!
(as only a cat can)
My throat screamed
but my face held an unexpected smile.

I felt a chill of admiration
or, maybe, amazement,
for the lazy, leashed housecat
whose muscles suddenly peaked,
rippled and
shot his white body through the grass
towards a tiny mouse.
I raced my heartbeat
to where he sat
pawing his catch,
screaming for him to stop.
He looked at me with rock eyes
as if daring me to interfere,
taunting.

The shrieks of the mouse
ached in my ears
springing me
to grab my beloved cat,
my feline brother,
who ignored my strikes.
I felt the surge of the struggle
electrify me.
I shook the growling butcher
by his hind quarters.
The mouse, still dangling from his lips,
uttered strangled screeches.
I pinned the cat down
and tried to force his mouth open.
He growled;
I screamed.
I was charged with the power I felt.
I saw victory edging my way when
